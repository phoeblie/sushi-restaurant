California Roll Recipe

A Delicious Fusion of Fresh Flavors and Sushi Tradition, Perfect for Any Palate.

Save this recipe

A California roll is one of the most iconic and accessible sushi rolls, particularly popular in Western countries, especially in the United States. Unlike traditional Japanese sushi, which often features raw fish, the California roll offers a unique twist by incorporating more familiar, cooked ingredients. This makes it appealing to those who are new to sushi or hesitant to try raw fish.

Ingredients

For the Sushi Rice:

  • 2 cups sushi rice (short-grain rice)
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the California Roll:

  • 4 sheets of nori (seaweed)
  • 1 cup imitation crab meat (or real crab, shredded)
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, peeled, seeded, and cut into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise (optional, for crab mixture)
  • Toasted sesame seeds or tobiko (fish roe) for garnish (optional)

Equipment:

  • Bamboo sushi rolling mat
  • Plastic wrap
  • Sharp knife

Make Sushi Rice & Crab Filling

  1. Rinse the sushi rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. In a medium pot, combine the rice and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes or until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  3. While the rice is cooking, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl until dissolved. Mix the imitation crab meat with mayonnaise in a bowl to create a creamy filling, set aside.
  4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl. Pour the vinegar mixture over the rice and gently fold it in with a spatula to season the rice. Let the rice cool to room temperature.

Assemble the California Rolls

  1. Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat on a clean surface and cover it with plastic wrap (to prevent sticking). Place a sheet of nori (shiny side down) on the plastic wrap.
  2. Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking and spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top edge of the nori bare. Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds or tobiko over the rice if using. Carefully flip the nori over so the rice is now on the bottom and the nori side is facing up.
  3. About 1 inch from the bottom edge, place a few slices of avocado, cucumber strips, and a small amount of crab mixture in a horizontal line.

Roll the Sushi

  1. Using the bamboo mat, begin rolling the sushi tightly from the bottom, making sure to keep the ingredients in place. Gently squeeze the mat as you roll to form a tight, compact roll.
  2. Once rolled, remove the sushi roll from the mat and place it on a cutting board.
  3. Using a sharp, wet knife, slice the sushi roll into 6-8 equal pieces.

Arrange the California rolls on a plate, garnishing with extra sesame seeds or tobiko if desired. Serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi.

Enjoy your homemade California sushi rolls!
